Participant Management Workflow

This workflow describes how event organizers manage participants for a large event. It also outlines what is expected from participants from the time they submit an application until the event takes place.

  1. Application: When a group has been invited to an event, the application becomes available through the My Applications feature in XXX. Applicants complete an application for the event and indicate the group they will be traveling with. A group may consist of family members, friends, or other participants. One member is designated as the group contact. Once the application is submitted, designated reviewers evaluate and approve it.
  2. Participant Selection: Event administrators review applications and select participants. Once selected, an email notification is sent to all approved participants. Approved participants are then granted access to the My Trip feature in XXX.
  3. Provide Additional Information: Using My Trip, participants review the information submitted during the application process and provide any additional travel or event details that are required.
    Note: Event organizers may also assist participants by entering or updating information on their behalf.
  4. Special Accommodation Requests: If participants request special accommodation arrangements and those requests are approved, room groups can be created for the affected participants. Organizers may also create room groups to divide approved participants among multiple rooms. Once a room group has been assigned, availability settings cannot be modified.
  5. Accommodation Assignments: When accommodations such as private lodging or hotel rooms become available, they can be assigned to room groups. Once accommodations are assigned, approved participants can view their lodging information.
  6. Group Accommodation Details: The group contact enters accommodation information for the group. Group members then provide any remaining itinerary information. Participants are given a deadline to complete all required details.
  7. Confirmed Participant: Participants are marked as confirmed once arrival information or check-in and check-out dates have been entered. This information should be provided before the specified deadline. Confirmation status may also be updated manually.
  8. Ready for Scheduling: Participants are considered ready for scheduling when they are confirmed, their accommodations are linked to a pickup or meeting location, and their availability and preferences have been entered. Once preferences are available, activities can be scheduled.
    Note: Participants with approved special accommodation arrangements are also considered ready for scheduling once their accommodations are linked to a pickup or meeting location.
  9. Itinerary Complete: Each participant must complete itinerary information such as check-in and check-out dates, arrival and departure details, availability, and activity preferences. Participants should verify that all information is accurate and complete.

    Participants may update their information or cancel their trip at any time. Those assigned special accommodations may review their accommodation details. If a group contact wishes to cancel, the group contact role must first be transferred to another member of the group.
  10. Review Information: After the submission deadline has passed, organizers review participant data to ensure all required information has been completed and verified.
  11. Event Management: During the event, organizers can review participant and event details using the Participant Management feature. Participants can review their travel and event information using My Trip in XXX.
